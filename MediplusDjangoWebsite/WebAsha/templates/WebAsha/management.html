Diabetes Management

Managing diabetes involves controlling blood sugar levels and preventing complications through lifestyle changes and medical interventions.

Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1 Diabetes: An autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little to no insulin.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: A condition where the body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Diabetes that develops during pregnancy and typically resolves after delivery.

Management Strategies

Effective diabetes management includes:

  • Blood Sugar Monitoring: Regular checks to manage glucose levels.
  • Medications: Insulin or oral medications to control blood sugar levels.
  • Lifestyle Modifications: Healthy eating, regular exercise, and weight management.

Preventive Measures

To reduce the risk of diabetes complications:

  • Adopt a balanced diet and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Engage in physical activity regularly.
  • Monitor and manage blood glucose levels effectively.
  • Attend regular medical check-ups and screenings.
